About the Lab

Connecting neural activity, perception, and imaging signals.

The Brain Imaging Signals Lab studies the neural basis of visual perception and the physiological origins of brain imaging signals. The lab combines brain imaging, electrophysiology, computation, and visual neuroscience to connect measured signals with underlying neural mechanisms.

Research Areas

Computational Neuroimaging and AI

We develop and apply advanced computational models and machine learning algorithms to interpret large-scale neuroimaging datasets. Our work includes using AI to identify MRI contrast, automate data processing pipelines, and build predictive models of brain disease, with a focus on Parkinson’s and Alzheimer’s disease.

Origins of fMRI Signals

Our lab investigates the neuronal and vascular mechanisms that underlie the BOLD (Blood Oxygen Level Dependent) signal used in functional MRI. By combining high-resolution fMRI with electrophysiology and optical imaging, we have demonstrated that negative BOLD signals are associated with decreases in neuronal activity, and that spontaneous fluctuations in fMRI reflect underlying neural connectivity.

Visual Perception and Organization

We study how the visual cortex is organized and how it represents information. Using ultra-high-field 7 Tesla MRI, we are able to decode fine-scale structures like ocular dominance columns and orientation maps in the human brain, providing insights into how we perceive the world.

Dr. Amir Shmuel, Director

Principal Investigator

Dr. Amir Shmuel

Professor of Neurology and Neurosurgery, and Director of the Brain Imaging Signals Lab at McGill University. Dr. Shmuel is a world leader in understanding the neuronal basis of functional brain imaging signals.

His work has demonstrated the neural basis of negative BOLD signals and elucidated the relationship between spontaneous fluctuations in fMRI and neuronal activity.
